Predictive Lead Scoring Agent

Executive Summary

The Predictive Lead Scoring Agent transforms the traditional rules-based lead qualification process into an AI-driven, continuously learning system that scores every inbound lead against historical conversion patterns, firmographic attributes, and real-time behavioral signals. By leveraging D365 Sales Insights and AI Builder predictive models, the agent replaces subjective "gut-feel" qualification with data-backed probability scores, ensuring sales reps focus their finite selling time on leads most likely to convert.

Unlike static BANT-based scoring, the Predictive Lead Scoring Agent adapts to shifting market dynamics by retraining its models on a rolling window of closed-loop outcomes. When a lead converts to an opportunity or is disqualified, that signal feeds back into the model, progressively improving accuracy. The agent also identifies "sleeper" leads — those that score low on traditional criteria but exhibit digital engagement patterns (repeated website visits, content downloads, webinar attendance) that correlate with eventual purchase intent.

For sales leadership, the agent provides transparency into pipeline quality at the top of the funnel. Managers can compare predicted lead quality distributions across campaigns, territories, and time periods, enabling sharper marketing spend allocation and territory planning decisions. The result is a measurable reduction in lead response time, a higher lead-to-opportunity conversion rate, and more efficient utilization of the sales team's highest-value resource: selling hours.

Business Case & ROI

Problem Statement

Sales reps spend an estimated 40-50% of their time on leads that will never convert, while high-potential leads languish unworked because manual scoring fails to surface them quickly. Marketing and sales teams lack a shared, data-driven definition of lead quality, causing friction at the MQL-to-SQL handoff.

Current State

  • Lead scoring relies on static rules (job title, company size, industry) that produce high false-positive rates.
  • Average lead response time exceeds 24 hours because reps lack prioritization guidance.
  • Only 20-25% of MQLs are accepted by sales as genuinely qualified (SQL conversion rate).
  • Lead data is incomplete — 35% of records lack firmographic enrichment.

Proposed State

  • AI-driven scoring assigns a 0-100 probability score to every lead within minutes of creation.
  • Leads above the 75th percentile are auto-routed to reps with < 4-hour SLA.
  • Model retrains weekly using closed-loop conversion outcomes.
  • Firmographic gaps are auto-filled via Bing Entity Search and LinkedIn Sales Navigator enrichment.

Agent Architecture & Design

Agent Type: Autonomous (event-driven with scheduled batch)

Confidence Threshold: Score > 75 = auto-route; 40-74 = rep review; < 40 = nurture queue

Input Sources

  • D365 Sales Lead entity (demographics, source, campaign)
  • D365 Marketing behavioral events (email opens, clicks, page visits)
  • LinkedIn Sales Navigator profile data (role, seniority, company signals)
  • Bing Entity Search API (company financials, news, headcount)
  • Historical closed-loop data (lead → opportunity → won/lost outcomes)

Processing Logic

  1. New lead created or updated → event triggers scoring pipeline.
    1. Feature extraction: compile 45+ features across firmographic, demographic, behavioral, and engagement categories.
      1. AI Builder prediction model scores the lead (0-100 probability).
        1. Score is compared against configurable thresholds (high / medium / low).
          1. Enrichment sub-agent fills any missing firmographic fields.
            1. Lead is tagged with score, score tier, and top-3 scoring factors.
              1. Routing rules execute: high → auto-assign to rep; medium → queue for review; low → nurture sequence.
                1. Score, features, and routing decision are logged for audit and retraining.

                Output Actions

                • Update Lead record: leadqualitycode, custom predictivescore field, scorefactors JSON.
                • Create Task or Phone Call activity for high-score leads with SLA deadline.
                • Push notification to rep via D365 Sales mobile app or Teams.
                • Publish score-change event to Dataverse event bus for downstream consumers.

                Feedback Loop

                • When a lead converts to an opportunity → positive training signal captured.
                • When a lead is disqualified → negative training signal captured.
                • Weekly model accuracy evaluation; automatic retraining triggered if accuracy drops below 80%.
                • Monthly human review of top-10 false positives and false negatives to identify feature gaps.

Executive Summary

The Deal Prioritization Agent addresses one of the most persistent challenges in sales execution: deciding which opportunities deserve attention right now. In a typical enterprise sales org, reps manage 30-60 active opportunities simultaneously, each at different stages, with varying deal sizes, competitive dynamics, and urgency levels. Without intelligent prioritization, reps default to recency bias (working whatever came in last) or squeaky-wheel syndrome (responding to whoever follows up most aggressively), leaving high-value, winnable deals under-worked.

This agent constructs a composite priority score for every open opportunity by blending four weighted dimensions: AI-predicted win probability, weighted deal value, strategic account alignment, and time sensitivity (days to close date relative to stage velocity benchmarks). The result is a dynamic, personalized "Top 10" work list that updates in real-time as deal signals change. When a competitor is added to a deal, when a key stakeholder goes silent, or when a close date slips — the priority stack reshuffles instantly.

For sales managers, the agent provides team-level priority views that highlight coverage gaps (high-priority deals with no recent activity) and resource conflicts (multiple high-priority deals competing for the same specialist resources). This enables proactive deal coaching rather than reactive firefighting, shifting management from "what happened" to "what should happen next."

  • 30% of deals in the top-half of the pipeline receive zero rep activity in any given week.
  • Pipeline reviews are backward-looking, reviewing what happened rather than prescribing action.
  • Win rates on deals > $100K are 15% lower than deals < $50K due to insufficient attention.

Proposed State

  • Every open opportunity carries a real-time composite priority score (0-100).
  • Reps receive a daily "Top 10" action list ranked by priority with specific recommended actions.
  • Managers see team-level priority heatmaps highlighting coverage gaps and at-risk deals.
  • Priority scores auto-adjust as deal signals change throughout the day.

Input Sources

  • Stakeholder engagement data (contact interactions, relationship health scores)

Processing Logic

  1. Deal signal change detected (stage move, activity logged, field update) → scoring triggered.
    1. Retrieve current opportunity data and associated entity relationships.
      1. Calculate four sub-scores:
      2. Win Probability (0-25): from predictive model output.
      3. Weighted Value (0-25): deal amount × stage probability, normalized to portfolio.
      4. Strategic Alignment (0-25): named account tier, cross-sell potential, logo value.
      5. Time Sensitivity (0-25): days to close vs. stage velocity benchmark; urgency multiplier.
        1. Apply business-unit-specific weighting configuration to produce composite score (0-100).
          1. Rank all open opportunities for each rep; identify Top 10.
            1. Generate recommended next action for each Top 10 deal based on current stage and gaps.
              1. Persist scores and ranking; push to rep work list in Sales Accelerator.
                1. Flag coverage gaps (Top 10 deals with no activity in 5+ days) for manager alerts.

                Output Actions

                • Update custom priorityscore, priorityrank, recommendedaction fields on Opportunity.
                • Refresh Sales Accelerator work list sequence for each rep.
                • Send Teams adaptive card with daily Top 10 summary at start of business.
                • Push manager alert for deals with priority > 80 and no activity in 5+ days.

                Feedback Loop

                • Closed deals (Won/Lost) feed back to validate whether priority ranking correlated with outcomes.
                • Monthly analysis: "Did reps who followed the priority list win more?" → adjust weightings.
                • Rep feedback mechanism: "Was this priority ranking helpful?" thumbs up/down on daily digest.
                • Quarterly weighting calibration review with revenue ops.

Executive Summary

The Opportunity Risk Alert Agent serves as an always-on early warning system for the sales pipeline, continuously monitoring dozens of risk signals across every open opportunity and alerting reps and managers before deals deteriorate beyond recovery. In most sales organizations, deal risk is identified reactively — during weekly pipeline reviews when a manager notices a stalled deal, or worse, when a customer goes dark and the rep finally updates the CRM. By then, the window for intervention has often closed.

This agent inverts that dynamic by applying real-time risk detection across five signal categories: engagement decline (fewer emails, calls, meetings), milestone slippage (overdue stage transitions, missed deadlines), stakeholder instability (champion departure, new decision-maker, organizational restructuring), competitive pressure (competitor mentions in communications, battle-card triggers), and sentiment deterioration (negative tone in email exchanges, meeting transcript analysis via Conversation Intelligence).

Each opportunity receives a dynamic risk score (0-100) that updates as signals change. When the score crosses configurable thresholds, the agent generates targeted alerts with specific recovery recommendations — not just "this deal is at risk," but "stakeholder engagement has dropped 60% in 14 days; schedule a re-engagement call with the economic buyer and prepare a value reinforcement deck." This prescriptive approach transforms risk alerts from noise into actionable coaching moments.

  • 40% of lost deals showed detectable risk signals 3+ weeks before the loss was recorded.
  • Pipeline reviews focus on top-line metrics (stage, amount, close date) rather than leading indicators.
  • Reps update deal health subjectively; manager visibility into actual engagement is limited.

Proposed State

  • Continuous risk monitoring across 5 signal categories for every open opportunity.
  • Automated alerts within 48 hours of risk signal detection with prescriptive recovery actions.
  • Manager risk dashboard showing team-wide risk distribution and trending.
  • Integration with Conversation Intelligence for sentiment-based risk detection.

Processing Logic

    3. Milestone Slippage (0-20): days overdue in current stage vs. historical velocity.

  1. As a CMO, I want weekly attribution reports showing marketing channel ROI and recommended budget shifts so that I can reallocate spend from low-performing to high-performing channels without waiting for quarterly reviews.
  1. As a sales manager, I want attribution data that credits sales activities (calls, demos, field visits) alongside marketing activities so that the complete revenue picture includes both marketing-generated and sales-generated influence.
  1. As a revenue operations analyst, I want the ability to compare attribution models (first-touch, last-touch, linear, data-driven) side-by-side so that I can validate the data-driven model and build stakeholder trust.
  1. As a trade marketing manager, I want attribution for offline touchpoints (trade shows, in-store promotions, field visits) so that I can justify trade marketing spend with revenue-backed data.
  1. As a VP of Sales, I want pipeline and revenue attribution by campaign source so that I understand which marketing investments generate the most sales-ready opportunities.
  1. As a marketing analyst, I want attribution model accuracy metrics (holdout testing, incrementality validation) so that I trust the model's credit allocation and can defend budget recommendations.

Agent Architecture & Design

Agent Type: Autonomous (batch processing with periodic model retraining)

Model Type: Shapley value-based multi-touch attribution (cooperative game theory)

Attribution Window: 180 days (retail/CPG buying cycles can span 2-6 months)

Input Sources

  • D365 Sales Opportunity entity (closed-won revenue, product, customer, timeline)
  • D365 Marketing Campaign entity (campaign type, channel, spend, timeline)
  • Activity entity (emails, calls, meetings, demos — timestamped per opportunity)
  • Event/trade show attendance data (registrations, booth visits, follow-ups)
  • Digital marketing data (ad impressions, clicks, landing page visits — via UTM tracking)
  • Content engagement data (downloads, webinar attendance, video views)
  • Field sales visit logs (in-person visits to retailer/distributor accounts)
  • Customer journey touchpoint timeline (all interactions sorted chronologically)

Processing Logic

  1. For each closed-won opportunity, reconstruct the complete touchpoint timeline.
    1. Map each touchpoint to a channel and campaign.
      1. Calculate Shapley values for each touchpoint:
      2. For each possible subset of touchpoints, calculate the marginal contribution of adding each touchpoint.
      3. Average marginal contributions across all possible orderings.
      4. Result: proportional revenue credit for each touchpoint.
        1. Aggregate touchpoint-level credits to channel level and campaign level.
          1. Calculate channel ROI: attributed revenue / channel spend.
            1. Identify high-performing channels (ROI > 4x) and underperforming channels (ROI < 1.5x).
              1. Generate budget reallocation recommendations: shift $X from low-ROI to high-ROI channels.
                1. Run incrementality validation: compare attributed lift against control groups where available.
                  1. Publish attribution results to dashboards; update weekly.

                  Output Actions

                  • Update Opportunity record with attributed touchpoint credits.
                  • Publish channel-level and campaign-level attribution to Power BI dashboards.
                  • Generate budget reallocation recommendations for marketing leadership.
                  • Create weekly attribution digest for sales and marketing leadership.
                  • Provide campaign-level ROI data for marketing planning tools.

                  Feedback Loop

                  • A/B test attribution model against simpler models (first-touch, last-touch, linear).
                  • Incrementality testing: run controlled experiments to validate attribution credits.
                  • Monthly model retraining with latest closed-deal and touchpoint data.
                  • Quarterly stakeholder review: does attribution align with qualitative channel knowledge?

Implementation Process

Phase 1: Discovery & Data Integration (Weeks 1-5)

  • Inventory all touchpoint data sources: CRM activities, marketing campaigns, digital ads, events.
  • Map data integration requirements for each source (APIs, ETL, manual upload).
  • Build touchpoint data warehouse in Azure Synapse with unified schema.
  • Reconstruct 12 months of historical customer journeys for model training.
  • Establish baseline: current attribution methodology, marketing spend by channel.

Phase 2: Build & Train Model (Weeks 6-11)

  • Implement Shapley value attribution model in Azure ML.
  • Build touchpoint journey reconstruction pipeline.
  • Calculate channel-level and campaign-level attribution for historical data.
  • Develop Power BI attribution dashboards with channel ROI and campaign views.
  • Build budget reallocation recommendation engine.
  • Implement model comparison views (data-driven vs. first-touch vs. last-touch).

Phase 3: Pilot & Validate (Weeks 12-16)

  • Deploy attribution model on most recent quarter of data.
  • Validate against known channel performance (do results align with qualitative knowledge?).
  • Run incrementality test on one controllable channel (e.g., pause email for segment).
  • Present attribution results to marketing and sales leadership for feedback.
  • Tune model parameters based on stakeholder input and validation results.

Phase 4: Scale & Optimize (Weeks 17-24)

  • Roll out weekly attribution reporting to all marketing and sales teams.
  • Implement monthly budget reallocation cycle based on attribution insights.
  • Activate monthly model retraining with updated closed-deal data.
  • Establish quarterly attribution review cadence with CMO, VP Sales, and Rev Ops.
  • Publish "Data-Driven Marketing Investment" playbook with attribution best practices.
